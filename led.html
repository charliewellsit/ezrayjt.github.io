Light Emitting Diode (LED)


LED lamps are highly energy-efficient and long-lasting electric lights that utilize light-emitting diodes (LEDs) for illumination.

They outperform incandescent and fluorescent lamps with some commercially available LEDs achieving efficiencies over 200 lumens per watt.

LED lamps require electronic circuits for operation and are compatible with dimmers designed for incandescent lamps.

LEDs offer instant brightness, are durable with no impact on lifespan from frequent switching and may be used as a replacement for traditional lamps.



The dataset for LED bulbs is currently unavailable as they are not obligated to adhere to Minimum Energy Performance Standards and are not required to display an Energy Rating Label.

A consultation process is in progress to consider the implementation of Minimum Energy Performance Standards for LEDs.
