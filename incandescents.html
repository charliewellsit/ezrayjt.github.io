Incandescent Lamp


An incandescent lightbulb, also known as an incandescent lamp, emits light by heating a wire filament until it glows.

Encased in a glass bulb filled with vacuum or inert gas, the filament is supplied with current via embedded terminals or wires.

Despite their widespread use in household and commercial lighting, they are highly inefficient, converting less than 5% of energy into light, with the rest being emitted as heat.

Incandescents have shorter lifespans compared to other lighting options and are gradually being phased out by some governments to save energy.
